Robustifying point cloud networks by refocusing

3DV 2025

Meir Yossef Levi
Guy Gilboa
Technion
ArXiv Code

Abstract

Point-cloud networks often misplace their attention, latching onto spurious or background structures and thus failing under corruption or incompleteness. We propose a simple refocusing strategy: rather than designing new explainability tools, we harness existing saliency methods to reveal a modelâ€™s biases and use them to guide training. By suppressing overconfident yet uninformative regions, this approach improves robustness across diverse corruptions and partial inputs. Experiments on ModelNet-C and ScanNet show consistent gains in mCE and accuracy, with no architectural changes and minimal overhead.

Key Insights

Misplaced Attention

Point-cloud networks often focus on background or spurious regions, leading to brittle predictions under noise or incompleteness.

Refocusing via Saliency

We use existing explainability tools to reveal where the network attends, then guide training to suppress overconfident but uninformative regions.

Robust Feature Learning

By discouraging reliance on spurious cues, the model learns features tied to object structure, improving generalization to corrupt or partial data.

Plug-and-Play Gains

The refocusing strategy boosts robustness on ModelNet-C and ScanNet with no architectural changes and only minor training overhead.
